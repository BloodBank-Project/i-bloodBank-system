PATIENT GUIDELINES
  • Patients or their authorized representatives must provide informed consent before receiving a blood transfusion
  • Proper patient identification procedures must be followed to ensure that the correct blood product is administered to the intended recipient.
  • Comprehensive documentation of the blood transfusion process is essential for maintaining accurate medical records.
  • Blood transfusions are typically prescribed based on medical necessity, such as acute blood loss, anemia, or other medical conditions requiring augmentation of the blood's oxygen-carrying capacity.
  • Patients receiving blood transfusions are closely monitored for signs of adverse reactions, such as fever, chills, rash, shortness of breath, chest pain, or changes in blood pressure
